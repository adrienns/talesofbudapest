# Business Development & Funding Plan — Tales of Budapest

Researched 2026-07-11 (web-verified; anything marked **verify directly** could
not be fully confirmed online). Companion to
[PROJECT_BLUEPRINT.md](PROJECT_BLUEPRINT.md) (strategy),
[KILLER_APP_PLAN.md](KILLER_APP_PLAN.md) WS-P (partnership tasks), and
[COMPETITOR_LANDSCAPE.md](COMPETITOR_LANDSCAPE.md) (positioning). Grant
programs churn constantly — re-verify every deadline before acting; most
Hungarian calls are annual and the next cycle's dates shift by weeks.

**One-line conclusion:** the money follows the legitimacy, not the other way
around — spend July–September 2026 converting the historian+builder profile
into institutional endorsements (Budapest Brand, Erzsébetváros, Mazsihisz,
OSZK/Fortepan), then harvest the November 2026–May 2027 grant season
(Pro Cultura Urbis, NKA, ECHOES 3rd call, Rothschild 2027, district calls)
with letters of support already in hand.

**The structural fact this plan is built on:** almost every Hungarian grant
cycle runs winter→spring (calls open Nov–Feb, deadlines Jan–May). July 2026
sits in the *dead zone* — nearly everything for 2026 has closed, and the 2027
windows open in ~4 months. That is not bad luck; it is a gift: the summer is
exactly the time to build the endorsement network that Hungarian cultural
juries weight so heavily, and to get the legal entity and pilot demo ready.

## 1. Who is asking for money — the asset inventory

Funders don't fund apps; they fund credible people with proof. What Adrienn
brings to every table:

1. **The dual profile.** A Hungarian historian (source criticism, archival
   literacy, academic credibility) who is *also* the full-stack/AI engineer
   building the product. Cultural juries see "not AI slop — a historian
   checks every fact"; innovation juries see "solo founder who has already
   built the pipeline, zero burn". Almost nobody in cultural tech has both.
2. **Working software** — ingest pipeline, knowledge-graph schema, narrative
   + audio generation, map app. Demoable today. Most cultural-grant
   applicants bring a PDF; she brings a phone.
3. **Near-zero cost base** (blueprint §4) — a 3–5 M HUF grant is *runway*,
   not a rounding error. Small grants that VC-track startups ignore are
   material here.
4. **A first vertical with a natural constituency** — the Dohány Street
   Synagogue / District VII Jewish quarter, which maps onto dedicated
   funders (Rothschild Foundation, Mazsök) and a district that brands
   itself the *Kultúrnegyed* (Cultural Quarter).

## 3. The entity decision (blocking — decide by August)

Three viable forms; the grant tables above show why this can't be deferred:

| | Egyéni vállalkozó (EV) | Kft. | Nonprofit (egyesület / nonprofit Kft.) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Setup cost/time | free–trivial, days | ~100 k HUF + lawyer, 1–2 weeks; 3 M HUF nominal capital (not cash-blocked) | weeks–months (esp. egyesület: 10 founders needed) |
| Tax reality | átalányadó/KATA-class simplicity; cheapest possible | corporate accounting, ~30–50 k HUF/month bookkeeping | strictest reporting |
| App Store / revenue | fine (individual Apple account, F-01) | fine | awkward for a commercial app |
| NKA / municipal cultural grants | some calls yes, many NGO-only | some calls | **widest access** |
| Rothschild, most heritage foundations | no | no | **required** |
| Hiventures / Startup Factory / VC | no | **required** | no |
| GDPR/licensing contracts with archives | works | works, looks more serious | works |

**Recommendation:** register as **egyéni vállalkozó now** (it unlocks EUIPO
SME Fund, BKIK membership, invoicing for pilot/B2B revenue, and several
cultural calls at near-zero cost), and **do not found a nonprofit**. For the
NGO-only money (Rothschild, Mazsök, civil calls), *partner instead*: the
applicant is Mazsihisz / a Jewish heritage NGO / a university department, and
Tales of Budapest is the named technical contractor–content partner in the
budget. This keeps her cap table and IP clean for the later Kft. conversion,
which should happen exactly when the first equity investor (Hiventures) or
Startup Factory incubator requires it — not before. **Have one session with
an accountant (könyvelő) before registering** — átalányadó thresholds and
VAT (alanyi adómentesség, 12 M HUF limit) depend on her personal situation;
this plan is not tax advice. Log the decision in `docs/DECISIONS.md` (F-09).

## 4. The bizdev playbook — whom to meet, in what order, and what each cares about

Hungarian cultural funding runs on institutional endorsement: an application
with a fővárosi partner letter and an archive cooperation agreement beats a
better-written application without them, almost every time. So the order of
operations is fixed: **legitimacy partnerships → letters of support → grant
applications**. Never the reverse.

### 4.1 Round one (July–August): the legitimacy anchors

1. **Budapest Brand Zrt. — the first meeting to book.** They own the city's
   tourism strategy, budapestinfo.hu, the Budapest Card, and they sign
   cooperation agreements as a matter of routine. Pitch (tourism version,
   §5c): a premium English-language product for the city's biggest visitor
   segment that makes Budapest look *smart*, costs them nothing, and gives
   their channels exclusive content. Ask for: a pilot listing, feedback on
   the Dohány walk, and — the real prize — a **letter of intent/support**
   naming them. Everything downstream (Pro Cultura Urbis, districts,
   EU calls) gets easier with the Főváros's own agency on record.
2. **Erzsébetváros (District VII) Kulturális és Oktatási Iroda** (Garay u.
   5). They brand the district as the Kultúrnegyed and have funded exactly
   this kind of cultural-tourism project (Katalizátor). Pitch: the Jewish
   quarter's history, told source-faithfully, driving respectful tourism
   deeper into the district than the party-quarter image. Ask: what's
   coming in the 2027 cultural budget, and a support letter for EU/heritage
   applications.
3. **Mazsihisz + Dohány Street Synagogue management.** This is content
   legitimacy, not money. A cited-history app touching Jewish heritage
   *without* community endorsement is a reputational landmine; *with* it,
   it's a Rothschild application. Pitch (cultural version, §5a): every fact
   carries an archival citation; the community reviews the narrative before
   publication; the app sends visitors to the synagogue's own ticketed
   tour, not around it. Ask: a content-review relationship now, and joint
   application to Rothschild Foundation spring 2027 (they or an affiliated
   NGO as applicant). Mazsök is the same milieu — one warm intro serves both.

### 4.2 Round two (September–October): the data partners

4. **Fortepan, Arcanum/Hungaricana, OSZK-MEK** — already partially engaged
   per the licensing workstream (WS-L). Upgrade the relationship from
   "license permission" to "named partnership": a one-page cooperation
   memo ("Tales of Budapest builds on X's collection, X is credited
   in-app") costs them nothing and is worth real points on NKA, ECHOES and
   Creative Europe evaluations. OSZK specifically matters for **ECHOES** —
   the 3rd cascading call (Jan 2027) favours cultural-heritage-institution
   consortia; OSZK or the Budapest City Archives as lead with Adrienn as
   technical partner is the strongest possible configuration.

### 4.3 Round three (October onward): academia and the guild

5. **ELTE / CEU digital humanities and history departments.** Not for
   money — for the jury room. Hungarian cultural evaluation committees are
   staffed from academia; being the practitioner who gave a guest talk on
   "LLM extraction over the 1888–1928 address books" makes her a known
   quantity. It also opens the NKFIH-research-partner route (§2a) and
   supplies the student beta-tester pipeline (WS-Q).
6. **Tour-guide associations — channel, not enemy.** Licensed Budapest
   guides will otherwise frame the app as the thing stealing their work.
   Flip it: offer the knowledge graph as a *research tool for guides*
   (B2B seats later), and recruit 2–3 respected guides as paid narrative
   reviewers. Their public blessing neutralises the "app vs. guide"
   story before a journalist writes it.

### 4.4 The visibility circuit (cheap, compounding)

- **Brain Bar, 17–18 Sep 2026, House of Music** — apply to speak/demo; the
  "historian who builds AI" angle is precisely their casting. Deadline for
  program slots: **verify directly**, likely already tight in July.
- Museum-tech / digital-humanities meetups (Múzeumok Éjszakája orbit — the
  June 2027 edition is a natural launch stage), Budapest smart-city events,
  and the Kreatív Európa Iroda's info days (they broker consortium
  invitations — attending is how you get invited as a partner).
- One good English blog post + conference talk: "Building a cited knowledge
  graph from 40 years of Budapest address books" travels far in both the
  heritage-tech and AI-engineering worlds and is the cheapest inbound-
  partnership magnet available.

### 4.5 What each funder type actually scores

- **Cultural juries (NKA, PCU, districts):** authenticity, institutional
  partners, public benefit, Hungarian language presence, feasibility.
  Weakness to pre-empt: "is this a business masquerading as culture?" —
  answer with the free tier and the citation ethos.
- **Innovation bodies (NKFIH, incubators, Hiventures):** team, defensibility,
  market, traction. Weakness: "solo founder" — answer with shipped product,
  near-zero burn, and the data moat nobody can hire their way into.
- **Tourism bodies (Budapest Brand, hotels):** visitor experience quality,
  city image, zero operational burden on them. Weakness: "another app" —
  answer with the archival depth no competitor has (COMPETITOR_LANDSCAPE
  one-liner) and English-first polish.
- **EU evaluators:** consortium quality, European added value, open access,
  sustainability plan. Weakness: solo micro-entity — answer by never
  applying alone; always inside or alongside a CHI.

## 5. Positioning — the three pitches

### 5a. To cultural bodies (historian first)

> Tales of Budapest is source-faithful heritage storytelling: every claim in
> every audio walk carries a citation to the archival page it came from —
> address books, digitized monographs, Fortepan photographs — assembled into
> a knowledge graph by a historian, not scraped by a chatbot. Where AI tour
> apps hallucinate, we footnote. The app makes the collections of Hungarian
> public institutions walkable for the public that will never open a
> research database, in Hungarian and English, with every source credited.

### 5b. To innovation/startup bodies (technologist first)

> Tales of Budapest runs an AI knowledge-graph pipeline over national
> archives: OCR and LLM extraction turn 1880–1928 address directories,
> historical books and photo archives into a structured, citation-preserving
> graph of buildings, residents and events — then generates narrated,
> geo-triggered walking tours from it. The corpus processing is one-time and
> batch; marginal cost per user is near zero. The moat is the verified graph:
> a competitor can copy the UI in a month, but not the archive partnerships
> and the historian-in-the-loop verification layer. Built and shipped by a
> solo technical founder.

### 5c. To tourism bodies (product first)

> Budapest's largest visitor segment is English-speaking cultural tourists,
> and what they get today is either a generic AI narrator or a static audio
> file. Tales of Budapest is a premium English-language product that lets a
> visitor stand in front of a building and hear who actually lived there —
> verified against Budapest's own archives — then follow those people across
> the city. It deepens dwell time in heritage districts, routes visitors to
> partner institutions' own programs, and makes Budapest the first city in
> Europe whose official story is checkable to the page.

### 5d. Founder bio template (reuse everywhere)
