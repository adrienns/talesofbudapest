# Competitor Landscape — Tales of Budapest

Researched 2026-07-10 (web-verified; items marked UNVERIFIED could not be
confirmed). Companion to [PROJECT_BLUEPRINT.md](PROJECT_BLUEPRINT.md) §2
(why this wins) and the moat strategy. Re-check the AI-tour cohort (§2
below) every ~6 months — it moves fastest.

**One-line conclusion:** nobody combines a cited knowledge graph
(buildings ↔ residents ↔ events), archival provenance in a consumer UX,
and the multimedia walking experience. The "cited, human-verified,
network-structured local history" position is empty — globally, not just
in Budapest. The threat is not an existing app; it is generic AI narration
becoming a free platform default, which is survivable only by owning what
LLMs can't fake: the verified graph.

## 2. AI-generated tour guides — the fast-moving cohort

- **SmartGuide** ([smartguide.app](https://www.smartguide.app/)) — Prague
  B2B SaaS + consumer app; AI TTS, translation, auto routes; ~€1M grant +
  ~$665k raised; premium €7.49/mo. **Has a dedicated Budapest guide.**
  Partial-to-direct on delivery; content is generic sight descriptions,
  no archival depth.
- **StoryHunt "Personal Tour Guide"** (launched Oct 2025) — LLM-generated
  personalized audio walks per interests/time budget, any city. Direct on
  personalization.
- **Narrativ** ([getnarrativ.app](https://getnarrativ.app/)) — AI themed
  tours on demand, "trained on historical sources," 100+ cities. Direct
  on themed-route generation.
- **Votura** ([votura.app](https://votura.app/)) — AI custom tours,
  500+ cities, offline, 12 languages.
- **VoxTour.ai** — publicly moved from raw GPT to RAG because of
  hallucinations; **Summer AI** markets a "proprietary fact-checking
  algorithm". The market knows accuracy is its weak point — and none of
  them shows verifiable sources.
- **Autio** (Kevin Costner, $5.9M) — US road-trip audio, expanding
  internationally; no Budapest. Adjacent.
- **Google "Talking Tours"** (Arts & Culture experiment) — Gemini
  narrates from Street View imagery, 55 landmarks. An experiment today;
  the platform-risk vector if it ever moves into Google Maps.

## 3. Budapest-specific

- **Budapest Time Machine** (Hungaricana/Arcanum,
  [hungaricana.hu](https://www.hungaricana.hu/en/databases/budapest-time-machine/)) —
  interactive historical maps + archival sources per plot, including
  residents. **The single closest thing to "building↔resident with
  citations" that exists** — but a desktop research web platform: no
  audio, no mobile UX, no narrative. Moat source, not competitor.
- **Budapest100** (KÉK + OSA, [budapest100.hu](https://budapest100.hu/en/about-us/)) —
  crowdsourced building/resident/story database + annual festival.
  Closest *content* analogue; a partner (already in the plan, P-11), not
  a competitor.
- **Fortepan / ForteGo** — free app geolocating Fortepan archival photos
  for then/now rephotography; hobby app, maintenance appears stalled
  (UNVERIFIED). Overlaps the photo-slider feature only.
- **BudapestGO** — BKK's official app is transit-only. No official
  Budapest history app surfaced.
- **Imagine Budapest** — well-reviewed *in-person* thematic tours; no app.

## 4. History / AR time-travel

- **Timelooper** (Hungarian-founded) — pivoted to B2B VR/AR for museums;
  not a Budapest consumer app anymore.
- **HistoPad** (Histovery) — tablet AR inside venues (B2B). Adjacent.
- **Time Portal** — 1M+ historical photos, then/now on location; global
  and shallow. Partial overlap with the slider feature.
- **AI talking historical figures** — Versailles runs conversational
  statues (OpenAI/Ask Mona); vendors sell "AI historical figures" to
  museums. The talking-resident idea is *validated*, but nobody does it
  street-level, city-wide, tied to real archival residents.

## 5. Knowledge-graph / "who lived here"

- **Historypin** — alive as a web platform, mobile apps discontinued
  since 2015. Semi-dormant.
- **HouseNovel** (US) — address-searchable home histories + past
  residents, crowdsourced; US-only, web, genealogy/real-estate audience.
  Closest product *concept*, different market entirely.
- **No consumer app anywhere** was found that models
  buildings↔residents↔events as a cited knowledge graph and delivers it
  as walking tours.

## Gap analysis

**(a) Closest competitors, by axis.**
- *Experience:* VoiceMap — quality dramatized geo-audio in Budapest
  today; static, linear, no visuals, no data layer.
- *Technology:* SmartGuide + the 2025 AI cohort (StoryHunt, Narrativ,
  Votura) — personalized AI routes and narration, Budapest covered by
  SmartGuide; content shallow, generic, citation-free.
- *Content:* Budapest Time Machine + Budapest100 + Fortepan collectively
  own the raw material — none packaged as a consumer mobile experience.
  That trio is the partnership pool and moat source.

**(b) The empty position nobody occupies.** The combination of:
1. a structured graph where residents connect buildings to each other
   (walk from the composer's flat to his mistress's salon);
2. archival citations surfaced in a consumer app — every AI competitor's
   known weakness is hallucination, and none offers verifiable sources;
3. the full multimedia stack (geo-audio + then/now sliders + animated
   resident portraits) in one product.

**(c) Biggest threat, next 2 years: commoditization, not competition.**
LLMs are collapsing the market for *generic* audio narration — SmartGuide
already covers Budapest, StoryHunt/Narrativ generate any-city tours in
seconds, and Google's Talking Tours shows "free AI walking tour" could
become a platform default. A solo developer cannot win on breadth or
distribution against that. The defensible response is the un-copyable
part: the curated, cited Budapest knowledge graph plus local
institutional relationships (Fortepan, OSA, Budapest100, Hungaricana)
that a global player won't replicate. **Secondary risk:** one of the AI
apps bolts a RAG layer onto Hungaricana/Fortepan's open data — the data
is public, so *speed to a polished, Budapest-native product matters.*

## What this means for the plan (already reflected)

1. The moat ranking in the blueprint stands: verified graph > exclusive
   institutional access > community memory > street presence > depth
   over breadth. This research confirms the position is empty and the
   graph is the defense.
2. Citations-in-the-UI (plan A-04) are not a nice-to-have — they are the
   *visible differentiator* against every AI competitor whose known
   weakness is hallucination. Market the sources, don't hide them.
3. Partnerships with the "content trio" (Budapest100 P-11, Fortepan P-14,
   Hungaricana-owning institutions L-16..L-18) double as competitive
   pre-emption: an official blessing makes the RAG-copycat route awkward.
4. The 6-month launch timeline has real urgency behind it now — see (c).
