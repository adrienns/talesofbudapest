## Copyright and publication record

- Competitor pages were inspected to measure popularity, duration, public stops and review themes. Their descriptions are not source material for the scripts.
- No competitor wording, guide joke, signature anecdote, map artwork, photograph, recording or review quotation appears in the manifests.
- Historical facts and public locations are researched independently. Hungarian copyright protects original expression rather than facts, ideas or methods; see S03 and `docs/licensing.md`.
- The current flagship card retains its existing Wikimedia image and visible credit. Per-stop images are intentionally unset until file-level licences, creators and required attribution can be shown in the player.
- Sensitive claims fail closed: future editors must add a source ID here before altering a script claim. Sources with restrictive or unknown reuse terms may be consulted for fact checking but their prose must not be ingested or paraphrased closely.

## Bilingual editorial notes

The Hungarian text is an independent adaptation with the same claims, stop order and source IDs. It uses Hungarian institutional names and natural sentence rhythm rather than mirroring English syntax. Hungarian’s agglutinative structure generally produces fewer whitespace-delimited words for equivalent spoken content. Both versions reserve roughly five minutes per stop for looking, photographs, a short rest or silence; combined with ~46 minutes of walking and the narration, the designed experience is approximately 110–140 minutes.
