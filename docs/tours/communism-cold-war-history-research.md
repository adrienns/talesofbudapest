# Communism & Cold War History Tour — research and production dossier

Research reviewed: 2026-07-19  
Tour manifest: `talesofbudapest-backend/content/curated/communism-cold-war-history.en.js`

## Editorial scope

The tour has nine chapters, each written for roughly three minutes at a measured documentary pace. Walking and observation bring the complete route to about 2 hours 45 minutes. The pedestrian route was checked with Valhalla/OpenStreetMap: 7.589 km and about 95 minutes of walking before stops.

The arc is deliberately chronological rather than comprehensive: Stalinist political policing; public propaganda; Imre Nagy; the 1956 Revolution; Cold War division and adaptation; repression; and the negotiated transition of 1989. The narration distinguishes the Arrow Cross regime from the postwar communist dictatorship and does not imply that the two systems were identical.

## Claim-control decisions

| Topic | Editorial decision | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Andrássy út 60 | State that the communist political police occupied it from 1945 and used cells/interrogation rooms; explicitly state it was not an official execution site. | The museum itself makes this correction. |
| Kossuth Square, 25 October 1956 | Do not state one exact casualty number or an uncontested sequence of shooters. | Sources and later investigations give conflicting reconstructions and totals. |
| Hungarian Radio | Describe the detention of the delegation and escalation into armed fighting; do not name a single, certain “first shooter.” | The broad sequence is documented; exact first-shot accounts conflict. |
| Imre Nagy | Describe him as a long-serving communist reformer who broke with Moscow in 1956, not as a lifelong anti-communist. | His political biography is central to understanding the revolution. |
| Soviet memorial | Pair the Red Army’s role in defeating Nazi Germany with the Soviet domination that followed. | Both are necessary; neither cancels the other. |
| “Goulash communism” | Use the label with a warning that relative consumer flexibility did not end one-party rule, censorship or surveillance. | Avoids nostalgia replacing institutional history. |
| Memorial surfaces | Distinguish current memorial design from surviving 1956 fabric. | Visitors should not mistake later interpretive elements for original evidence. |

No dialogue, composite character, exact crowd thought, invented sound, or unsupported private anecdote appears in the narration.

## Sound design and rights

The manifest contains non-spoken `audioDirection` for every stop plus tour-wide mixing rules. It uses the local CC0 master **“First Light Particles” by Yoiyami**, sourced from [OpenGameArt](https://opengameart.org/content/first-light-particles-%E2%80%93-cc0-atmospheric-pianoambient-track), as its sole sound-design asset. The offline mix uses a ten-second opening cue at -24 dB relative to narration, fading it out before walking directions. It is enabled only at Oktogon, Liberty Square and Astoria; the other six chapters remain dry.

No gunfire, scream, interrogation, execution, tank or panic reconstruction should be used. These effects are historically imprecise, can be distressing at memorial sites and turn documented deaths into spectacle.

No third-party sound effects, archival audio or recreated ambience are included. Silence is intentional at Kossuth Square and during accounts of execution and casualties.

## Model recommendations

For the research master, use OpenAI `gpt-5.6-sol` through the Responses API with retrieval, a source allow-list and a claim ledger. Use medium or high reasoning for the first researched draft and force the model to mark unsupported claims rather than fill gaps. After this workflow has an evaluation set, `gpt-5.6-terra` is the better cost/quality production option. Official model guidance: https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/guides/latest-model

For narration, the repository uses direct Gemini free-tier TTS with `gemini-3.1-flash-tts-preview` and the `Sulafat` voice. Prompt for measured documentary delivery, 118–125 words per minute, restrained emotion and careful pronunciation of Hungarian names. The free tier is quota-limited; terminal quota errors stop the job with a clear unavailable-audio result and never fall back to OpenRouter. OpenRouter remains an explicit paid override only. Any future voice change should use a blind listening test with the same chapters and pronunciation list.
