Budapest sits on the Danube, divided into Buda and Pest. Castle Hill rises above the river with medieval walls, royal palaces, and churches that survived sieges, fires, and world wars.

During the Austro-Hungarian era, grand boulevards, opera houses, and parliament buildings reshaped Pest into a imperial capital. Architects blended neo-Gothic, art nouveau, and eclectic styles across districts from Lipótváros to the Jewish Quarter.

The city carries layered memories of revolution, occupation, and renewal—from the 1848 uprising to the 1956 revolution and the quiet transformations of the late twentieth century.
